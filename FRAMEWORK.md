# Ancient Pathogen Selection & Cancer: A Unified Framework

## Core Thesis

Cancer and ancient pathogens face the exact same problem: how to survive inside a human body that's trying to kill them. They evolved the same solutions, and they exploit the same immune genes. The genes shaped by 10,000 years of plague, malaria, TB, and smallpox are the same genes that cancers now hijack to evade the immune system.

This framework — *mining evolutionary immune selection for undrugged therapeutic targets* — is supported by hard evidence at every step. ERAP2 is proof of concept. At least 5 additional targets share the same evidence base.

## The Three Steps of Immune Defense (and How Cancer Breaks Each One)

### Step 1: DETECTION — "Can the immune system see it?"

Cells chop up proteins into peptides, load them onto MHC-I molecules, and display them on the cell surface. T cells patrol the body reading these displays like security guards checking IDs.

| Gene | Selected By | What It Does | Cancer Connection |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ERAP2** | Black Death (+40% survival) | Trims peptides to correct length for MHC-I | Overexpression drives anti-PD-1 resistance; inhibition forces tumors to present novel antigens |
| **HLA variants** | Malaria, leprosy, plague, TB, smallpox | The MHC-I/II molecules — what "ID cards" you can display | 1 in 4 tumors delete HLA alleles to hide |
| **NOD2** | Leprosy | Detects intracellular bacterial fragments | Crohn's disease + colorectal cancer risk |

**Drug status**: Wide open. No ERAP2 drugs exist. This is the most upstream intervention point.

### Step 2: ACTIVATION — "Does the immune system attack?"

Once a T cell recognizes something suspicious, checkpoint molecules decide whether to activate or stand down.

| Gene | Selected By | What It Does | Cancer Connection |
|---|---|---|---|
| **CTLA-4** | Black Death | Brake pedal — dampens T cell activation | Ipilimumab (Yervoy) — billion-dollar cancer drug |
| **CCR5** | Smallpox | Chemokine receptor — guides immune cells | Maraviroc (HIV drug) now in cancer metastasis trials |
| **Interferon genes** | Smallpox | Alarm signal activating antitumor immunity | 25% of immunotherapy-resistant cancers have IFN defects |
| **TICAM2** | Black Death | TLR4 signaling — triggers innate activation | TLR agonists in cancer trials |

**Drug status**: Most validated space. Checkpoint inhibitors (anti-CTLA-4, anti-PD-1) exist. New checkpoints (NKG2A-HLA-E, controlled by ERAP) still emerging.

### Step 3: KILLING — "Can the immune system finish the job?"

Activated T cells and NK cells deploy cytotoxic mechanisms. The tumor either dies or resists.

| Gene | Selected By | What It Does | Cancer Connection |
|---|---|---|---|
| **G6PD** | Malaria | Controls oxidative stress / NADPH balance | G6PD-deficient people have 40% less GI cancer; tumors depend on G6PD |
| **SLC11A1** | Tuberculosis | Controls iron in macrophages | Determines M1 (anti-tumor) vs M2 (pro-tumor) macrophage state |
| **Duffy/DARC** | Malaria | Chemokine receptor — immune cell recruitment | Prostate cancer disparity in African Americans |

**Drug status**: G6PD mimetics and SLC11A1 modulators are completely undrugged.

## Historical Timeline: 10,000 Years of Pathogens Rewriting Human Immune DNA

### Era 1: Neolithic Revolution (~10,000-5,000 BC)

Farming began. Dense settlements + animal contact = first zoonotic disease explosion. TB from cattle, malaria from irrigated farmland, influenza from pigs/birds. Ancient DNA shows Europeans throttled their immune systems — farmers produced dramatically lower cytokine levels than hunter-gatherers.

**Modern cost**: Autoimmune disease risk starts climbing.

### Era 2: Bronze Age Steppe Migration (~3,000-1,000 BC)

Pastoralists swept into Europe with horses and new diseases. Early plague (pneumonic Y. pestis) confirmed in Britain 4,000 years ago. 1,000 ancient genomes study: bulk of immune gene adaptation occurred after the Bronze Age (<4,500 years ago). MS-associated HLA variants trace to steppe migration.

### Era 3: Classical Antiquity (~500 BC-500 AD)

Dense cities and trade networks: Plague of Athens (430 BC), Antonine Plague (165 AD, likely smallpox), Plague of Justinian (541 AD, confirmed Y. pestis). Justinian plague selected 6 immune genes — different lineage from Black Death, proving same pathogen can independently select different gene sets.

### Era 4: Medieval Period (500-1500 AD) — "The Great Filter"

**Black Death (1346-1353)**: Killed 30-60% of Europe. Klunk et al. 2022 Nature: ERAP2 (+40% survival), CTLA-4, TICAM2, ADAMTS7 all under strong selection.

**Leprosy**: NOD2/CARD15 selected — now Crohn's disease + colorectal cancer risk.

**Smallpox**: CCR5-Delta32 begins rising — now HIV resistance + cancer metastasis modulation.

### Era 5: Colonial & Industrial Era (1500-1900)

Cholera selects ABO blood type (pancreatic/gastric cancer risk) and CFTR carriers (cystic fibrosis). TB selects SLC11A1 (macrophage polarization in tumors). Malaria selects G6PD deficiency (40% reduced GI cancer), sickle cell, Duffy-negative phenotype (prostate cancer disparity).

### Era 6: Modern Pandemics (1900-Present)

CCR5-Delta32 (selected by smallpox centuries earlier) → HIV resistance. COVID-19 risk factors include Neanderthal chromosome 3 haplotype. Evolution's echoes keep surfacing in modern disease.

## ERAP2 Deep Dive: The Primary Target

### Why ERAP2 Is First

- Strongest human selection event ever documented (+40% survival during Black Death)
- Lives in the endoplasmic reticulum — connects to ER stress/UPR cancer pathway
- Controls Step 1 (detection) — most upstream and least crowded intervention point
- No drugs on market. First selective nanomolar inhibitors only discovered in 2022

### Current ERAP2 Cancer Research

| Group | Finding | Year |
|---|---|---|
| Serwold lab (Emory) | ERAP2 supports T cell recognition of 3 tumor epitopes (gp100, MART-1, Tyrosinase) | 2023 |
| Bhatt et al. (Immunity) | Inhibiting ERAP disrupts NKG2A-HLA-E checkpoint — distinct from PD-1/CTLA-4 | Nov 2024 |
| MOLT-4 leukemia study | ERAP2 inhibition forces cancer cells to present novel antigenic peptides | 2022 |
| Pancreatic cancer group | ERAP2 as biomarker for gemcitabine response + autophagy/stellate activation | 2022 |
| AACR nasopharyngeal | ERAP2 overexpression linked to anti-PD-1 resistance | 2025 |

### Why No ERAP2 Drug Exists Yet

1. **Selectivity** — ERAP1/ERAP2 share 50% sequence identity. Early inhibitors hit both.
2. **Druggability** — Only two chemical scaffolds explored, both low potency/selectivity.
3. **Complexity** — Effect depends on context (inhibit vs. modulate).
4. **Money followed known bets** — Checkpoint inhibitors were proven. ERAP2 lacked tool compounds until 2022.

### Key Reference Compounds

- **DG013A** — Known ERAP inhibitor, current benchmark
- **Camberlein 2022 KTGS inhibitors** — First selective nanomolar ERAP2 inhibitors

### The ER Stress Connection

ERAP2 lives in the ER. ER stress / UPR is a hot cancer target:
- Nature Reviews Cancer (2025) — major review on ER stress in anticancer immunity
- Tumors hijack UPR to suppress immune responses
- BiP CAR-T cells showing anti-tumor activity in leukemia models
- ERAP2 inhibition → ER stress → UPR activation → altered antigen presentation
