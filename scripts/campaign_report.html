The Story

We set out to boost potency of our 92aa ERAP2 binder to 40-50% inhibition for cancer immunotherapy. ERAP2 trims antigenic peptides off cancer cells — inhibiting it exposes more neoantigens to the immune system. What we discovered instead changed the entire design strategy.

Act 1: The Binder Isn't Selective

Added tryptophan plug mutations and tethered VAGSAF cargo. All 4 constructs failed. Then discovered the parent binder itself prefers ERAP1 over ERAP2 (0.604 vs 0.572). The binder was never selective — every modification made it worse.

Act 2: VAGSAF Isn't Selective Either

Pivoted to free VAGSAF peptides as standalone drugs. Tested 5 stabilized variants (cyclic, Pro-capped, extended, disulfide). All bind IRAP ≥ ERAP2. The original triple selectivity was under different conditions. Free VAGSAF is promiscuous across all M1 aminopeptidases.

Act 3: The Rigid Linker Changes Everything

C2 EAAAK — binder + rigid helical linker + VAGSAF — was the only dual-selective construct. The EAAAK helix constrains VAGSAF delivery to ERAP2's channel only. The binder provides selectivity, VAGSAF provides potency, the rigid linker is the glue.

Act 4: Fixing IRAP — The Hard Problem

IRAP selectivity was only 1.2x. Contact analysis revealed EAAAK lysines form contacts with IRAP → switched to EAAAA (1.4x). Negative design (3 point mutations) failed — binder re-docked on IRAP in new orientations.

Breakthrough: Aligned ERAP2/IRAP sequences (52% identical). Mapped all 35 binder interface contacts: 17 touch only conserved positions (cross-reactivity drivers), 4 touch only divergent positions (selectivity drivers). Used ProteinMPNN to redesign the 17 conserved positions while keeping divergent contacts fixed.

mpnn06 emerged: ERAP2=0.815, ERAP1=0.326 (2.5x), IRAP=0.454 (1.8x).

Key Insights

  1. Binder = selectivity, peptide = potency, rigid linker = glue. None works alone.
  2. Free VAGSAF is promiscuous. Binds all M1 aminopeptidases equally.
  3. Cyclization kills aminopeptidase selectivity. Substrate grooves expect extended peptides.
  4. Point mutations can't fix structural cross-reactivity. Need systematic interface redesign.
  5. Conserved vs divergent contact mapping is the right framework for selectivity engineering.
  6. ProteinMPNN is noisy — 1 of 10 designs improved IRAP selectivity. Run large batches.
